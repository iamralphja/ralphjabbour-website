About

The paradox is the point.

Ralph Jabbour is a Lebanese author and advisor based in Dubai. His work examines the beliefs, identities, and behavioral patterns people carry without knowing if they have chosen them, and what it costs to keep carrying them. Not as theory, but as a pattern he has spent years learning to read, first in organizations, then in people, eventually in himself.

He works with people at the point where something has to change, in how they think, how they lead, how they live, or simply in the story they've been telling themselves about who they are and what they're capable of. His work doesn't follow a formula. It follows the person. What he brings is the ability to see clearly what others are too close to see, and to say it in a way that lands without flinching. He is the author of The Rebellion of Life: Burn the Lie and the host of Letters from Rebellion.

What he offers is not a roadmap. It is a mirror, and the courage to look into it.

The Rebellion Project is the ongoing body of work that names this, exposes it, and refuses to make it comfortable.

The Book

The Rebellion of Life

Burn the Lie

Published 2025
Format Paperback · Digital
ISBN 9798293242559

You already know something is wrong. Not with your circumstances. With the whole thing. The way you're living, what you're chasing, who you've become to keep it all running. You can feel it, even when you can't name it. This book names it.

The fears you've been managing, the choices you've been avoiding, the version of yourself you've been performing, none of it was inevitable. It was built. One small surrender at a time. The Rebellion of Life is not a self-help book. It doesn't have a morning routine, a five-step framework, or a promise that everything will work out. What it has is the one thing most people spend their entire lives avoiding: the truth about the life they're actually living, who built it, and what it's costing them every day they keep pretending it fits. Seventeen chapters. No comfort. No shortcuts. Just the kind of clarity that changes how you see everything, including yourself.

From truth to cowardice, indecision to creativity, collapse to what comes after — the book moves through the full arc of an installed life being recognized and, chapter by chapter, refused. It ends where it must: not with instructions, but with a question.

If you're done pretending, this is where it starts. Not for a better life. A truer one.
